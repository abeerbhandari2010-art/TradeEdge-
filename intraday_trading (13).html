What is Intraday Equity?

Intraday equity trading means buying and selling stocks within the same trading session (9:15 AM – 3:30 PM IST). Positions must be squared off before market close. Brokers typically offer 5x–10x leverage on intraday positions, amplifying both gains and losses.

Beginner

Entry & Exit Timing

The first 15 minutes (9:15–9:30) are highly volatile — experienced traders wait. The 10:00–11:30 window is considered the most liquid and directional. Avoid holding positions after 3:00 PM as reversal risk increases ahead of close.

Strategy

Volume & Liquidity

Only trade stocks with high average daily volumes (>5 lakh shares). Low-liquidity stocks have wide bid-ask spreads that erode profits. Stocks in NSE's F&O segment are generally the most liquid for intraday purposes.

Beginner

MIS vs CNC Orders

Use MIS (Margin Intraday Square-off) order type for intraday positions. CNC (Cash and Carry) is for delivery. MIS orders are auto-squared off by brokers at 3:15–3:20 PM if you don't exit yourself, sometimes at unfavorable prices.

Strategy

Futures (F) Basics

Futures are contracts to buy/sell an underlying (Nifty, BankNifty, stocks) at a fixed price on expiry. Each lot has a fixed size (e.g., Nifty = 50 units). Intraday futures trading requires lower margin than overnight and carries significant leverage — a 1% move in Nifty can mean 5–10% on your margin.

Advanced

Options (O) Basics

Options give the right (not obligation) to buy (Call) or sell (Put). For intraday, traders buy OTM options for directional bets. Theta decay is brutal for option buyers — a flat day can wipe 20–30% of premium. Best for trending days with clear catalysts like RBI policy or earnings.

Advanced

Weekly Expiry Trading

Nifty and BankNifty have weekly expiries every Thursday. The last day sees aggressive theta decay and gamma explosion near-the-money. Many retail traders lose capital buying options on expiry day — it's a specialist game requiring quick exits and tight stops.

High Risk

Open Interest Analysis

OI (Open Interest) shows active contracts. Rising OI + rising price = bullish confirmation. Falling OI + rising price = short covering rally (less sustainable). The Max Pain level (price where maximum options expire worthless) acts as a magnet near expiry. Check NSE options chain daily.

Strategy

Opening Range Breakout

Mark the high and low of the first 15–30 minutes. A breakout above the high with volume signals bullish momentum; below the low signals bearish. This is the most popular intraday strategy in Indian markets. Set stop-loss at the opposite end of the range.

Strategy

VWAP Trading

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the benchmark for institutional traders. Price above VWAP = bullish bias; below = bearish. Buy dips to VWAP in uptrends, sell rallies to VWAP in downtrends. Most professional intraday strategies incorporate VWAP as a key level.

Strategy

Trend + Pullback

Identify the direction using EMA (9, 20) crossover on 5-minute charts. Wait for a pullback to the 20 EMA in an uptrend, then enter on a bullish candle confirmation. This trades with momentum while avoiding chasing — improving your risk/reward significantly.

Beginner

Support/Resistance Flip

When price breaks a strong support level and retests it from below (now acting as resistance), it's a high-probability short entry. The reverse (resistance becomes support) is a long entry. Combine with volume spike at the breakout for confirmation.

Strategy

The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1–2% of your trading capital on a single trade. If you have ₹1,00,000, maximum loss per trade = ₹1,000–₹2,000. Calculate your stop-loss first, then determine position size. Most traders blow up accounts by ignoring this rule.

Critical

Stop-Loss Discipline

Set your stop-loss BEFORE entering the trade — not after. Never move a stop-loss further away hoping for recovery. Use hard stop-loss orders (not mental stops). A stock with a "small" 3% stop-loss becomes a 15% loss if you keep adjusting. Honor your stop, always.

Critical

Daily Loss Limit

Set a maximum daily drawdown of 3–5% of capital. Once hit, stop trading for the day. Revenge trading after losses leads to the biggest account drawdowns. Professional traders treat daily stop-loss as sacred — it preserves capital to trade another day.

Critical

Risk/Reward Ratio

Only take trades with minimum 1:2 risk/reward (risk ₹1 to make ₹2). This means you can be right only 40% of the time and still be profitable. Most new traders take 1:1 or worse setups. A good setup has defined entry, stop-loss, and at least 2 targets before you enter.

Quick Reference
KEY CONCEPTS
📊

Lot Size

The minimum quantity you can trade in F&O. Nifty = 50 units, BankNifty = 15, FinNifty = 40. Buying one lot means you control the value of that many units.

⚡

Leverage

Intraday equities: 5–10x. F&O futures: 5–8x. Options: Potentially 50–100x (high risk). Higher leverage = higher potential returns AND losses.

🎯

Theta Decay

Options lose value every day due to time decay (theta). For option buyers, time is the enemy. For sellers, time is money. Maximum decay occurs in the last week before expiry.

📈

PCR Ratio

Put-Call Ratio = total Put OI ÷ total Call OI. PCR > 1.2 = oversold/bullish reversal likely. PCR < 0.8 = overbought/bearish reversal likely. Watch daily on NSE website.

🔄

Margin

SPAN + Exposure margin is required for F&O. SEBI's peak margin rules mean you need full margin throughout the day. Check your broker's margin calculator before trading.

💧

Liquidity

Always check bid-ask spread before trading. A ₹10 spread on a ₹100 option means you're starting 10% in the hole. Stick to ATM or near-ATM options for tighter spreads.

🕐

Market Hours

Pre-open: 9:00–9:15 AM. Regular session: 9:15 AM–3:30 PM. Post-market: 3:40–4:00 PM. F&O positions should ideally be squared off by 3:25 PM.

🛡️

Circuit Breakers

NSE halts trading if Nifty moves 10%, 15%, or 20% in a day. Individual stocks have 5%, 10%, or 20% circuit filters. F&O stocks generally have 20% circuit limits.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the minimum capital needed to start intraday trading? +

For equities, you can technically start with ₹5,000–₹10,000 using MIS (intraday) leverage. However, ₹50,000–₹1,00,000 is recommended so that position sizes are meaningful and you can manage risk properly. For F&O, the minimum margin for 1 lot of Nifty futures is approximately ₹1.2–1.5 lakh. Starting small and scaling up as you gain experience is always advisable.

Are trade suggestions on TradeEdge actual buy/sell signals? +

No. All trade suggestions generated on TradeEdge are AI-generated educational ideas based on common technical patterns and market conditions. They are not SEBI-registered investment advice. You must do your own analysis, verify current price levels with your broker, and apply your own risk management before placing any trade. Never trade based solely on any automated system.

What time should I place my intraday trades? +

The best intraday window is typically 10:00 AM – 12:30 PM and again 2:00 PM – 3:00 PM. Avoid the first 15 minutes (9:15–9:30 AM) due to high volatility and wide spreads. Never hold unhedged intraday positions after 3:15 PM — many brokers auto-square off MIS positions between 3:15 and 3:20 PM, sometimes at unfavourable prices.

How do I add custom symbols to the indices bar? +

Click the ✏ Edit button on the top indices bar. In the modal, you can search for any NSE symbol using the search box with autocomplete, add from the Quick Add presets, or type in any symbol directly. You can add up to 8 symbols. Changes are saved locally and persist across sessions. Click Save & Apply to update the bar.

What is the difference between Futures and Options? +

Futures obligate you to buy/sell the underlying at expiry — your profit or loss is unlimited in both directions. Options give you the right (not obligation) to buy (Call) or sell (Put) — as a buyer, your maximum loss is the premium paid, while gains can be large. Futures carry more margin; options can be bought with smaller capital but lose value due to theta (time decay) every day.

Why is India VIX important for intraday traders? +

India VIX measures expected market volatility over the next 30 days. VIX above 20 = high fear, wider moves expected — reduce position sizes and widen stops. VIX below 13 = low volatility, tighter ranges — breakout strategies work better. A sudden spike in VIX during the day signals institutional hedging and possible sharp moves in either direction.
